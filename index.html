Natural Disasters ▼

Hurricanes

Secure your property and prepare an emergency kit with water, non-perishable food, medications, and important documents in a waterproof container. Monitor updates from the National Hurricane Center and heed evacuation orders.

Earthquakes

During an earthquake, Drop, Cover, and Hold On. After the shaking stops, check for injuries and structural damage. Stay alert for aftershocks and follow local evacuation guidelines.

Floods

When flood warnings are issued, move to higher ground immediately. Avoid driving through floodwaters and listen to local emergency broadcasts for evacuation instructions.

Wildfires

Stay updated with local air quality reports and evacuation orders. Evacuate promptly if advised. Wear protective clothing, a mask, and have a fire extinguisher handy.

Medical Emergencies ▼

Bleeding

Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage and elevate the wound. If the bleeding is severe or does not stop, seek emergency medical assistance immediately.

Choking

If the person cannot breathe, perform the Heimlich maneuver. Stand behind them, wrap your arms around their waist, and give quick upward thrusts. Call 911 if the situation worsens.

Heart Attack

Call 911 immediately. Help the person sit down and remain calm. If they are conscious and not allergic to aspirin, have them chew a standard dose while awaiting emergency services.

General Body Pains & Injuries

For minor injuries or body aches, rest and apply ice to reduce swelling. If pain is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other symptoms, consult a medical professional immediately.

Safety Tips ▼

Emergency Preparedness

Build an emergency kit with essentials such as water, non-perishable food, first aid supplies, flashlights, batteries, and important documents. Regularly update your kit and practice evacuation drills.

Home Safety

Install and test smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms frequently. Keep a fire extinguisher accessible and ensure escape routes remain clear.

Workplace Safety

Familiarize yourself with emergency exits and assembly points at your workplace. Participate in fire drills and safety training. Keep emergency contact numbers readily available.

Community Resources ▼

Local Grants & Safety Programs

Check with local government and community organizations for grants and programs designed to improve emergency preparedness. Visit your city or county website for details.

Emergency Response Contacts

Keep an updated list of contacts for Police, Fire Department, Ambulance, Poison Control, and Disaster Relief. Save these numbers on your phone and maintain a printed copy at home.
